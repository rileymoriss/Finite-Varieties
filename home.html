Varities Over Finite Fields

Instructions

Enter an integral polynomial to plot it over a finite cyclic ring, \(\mathbb{Z}/m\mathbb{Z}\). The syntax is \(n*x\) for constants and x^n for exponentiation. e.g. 3*x^2. If the function is in one variable it will plot \(y = f(x)\), if the function is in two variables it will plot \(f(x, y) = 0\). Click and drag on the axis to select a region to zoom in on.

Facts about finite fields

Finite fields are determined by their cardinality, with all finite fields being of cardinality \(p^n\) for some prime \(p\). The multiplicative structure of \(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}\) is given by \(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}^* \cong \mathbb{Z}/(p^n - 1)\mathbb{Z}\). Because the additive group is a \(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}\) vector space it is clear that the additive structure is just \(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}^+ \cong \big( \mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}\big)^n\). This makes the most obvious way of plotting the elements of a finite field as tuples in the cyclic group of the same order, hence we dont really have a nice way of plotting in the plane any finite field other than the cyclic ones.
